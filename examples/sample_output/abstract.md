Scientific communication often bottlenecks during the transformation of research insights into well-structured manuscripts, a process that requires extensive manual editing and is both cognitively expensive and time-consuming. We introduce AutoDraft, an autonomous drafting system that applies iterative refinement principles to research paper writing. AutoDraft decomposes research theses into structured components, generates section-by-section drafts, performs self-critique using explicit evaluation criteria, and revises based on identified weaknesses. Our system employs a five-criterion rubric evaluating clarity, coherence, rigor, novelty, and structure. Evaluation demonstrates that AutoDraft produces drafts with structural coherence comparable to human-written first drafts while significantly reducing iteration time. By shifting the researcher's role from manual editing to defining constraints and evaluation standards, AutoDraft enables focus on higher-level scientific thinking. This work demonstrates the viability of autonomous iteration for structured research writing and opens pathways for systems that can handle the full manuscript development lifecycle.
