# Sample Research Thesis

## Title
Autonomous Iterative Drafting Systems for Scientific Communication

## Core Hypothesis
Autonomous research agents can significantly reduce the structural and iterative burden of transforming research ideas into publishable papers through decomposition, drafting, and critique loops.

## Problem Statement
Scientific communication bottlenecks often occur not at the ideation stage but during the laborious process of converting research insights into well-structured manuscripts. Traditional approaches require extensive manual editing cycles that are cognitively expensive and time-consuming.

## Proposed Approach
We propose AutoDraft, a system that applies autonomous iteration principles to research paper drafting. The system decomposes research theses into structured components, generates drafts section-by-section, self-critiques for logical and structural coherence, and revises based on explicit evaluation criteria.

## Expected Contribution
This work demonstrates that autonomous drafting systems can operate at a level of structural rigor comparable to human researchers while significantly reducing iteration time. We introduce explicit scoring rubrics for draft evaluation and show how feedback loops can improve paper coherence without human intervention.

## Significance
By shifting the human role from manual editing to defining drafting constraints and evaluation criteria, we enable researchers to focus on higher-level scientific thinking while delegating structural refinement to autonomous systems.
